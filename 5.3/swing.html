Swing of Tokyo

In the 2024-25 Advanced Object-Oriented Design (AOOD), we coded a semi-popular game known as King of Tokyo in Java.

FAQ

In stage one in our design process for this game, we paired up into groups of three and worked together on understanding the rules and creating our own game engine while also setting common standards with the rest of the class.

During stage two, we worked individually to design and prototype, but not code, a potential User Interface for our game engine. There were certain in-depth requirements we had to meet, such as taking in user input on certain game controls.

Finally, in stage three, we split off from our original groups of three into pairs to allow for ease of collaboration. During this stage, we selected the better design and built it in Java Swing. We then integrated/connected our backend (custom game engine) to our frontend (Swing UI).
